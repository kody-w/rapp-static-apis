    Why it's cool

    A browser tab does active sensing: it emits a known waveform, listens for reflections, and solves time-of-flight distance from raw samples using real CPython in WebAssembly. Turn your body and each ping lands at a new bearing, so the map grows from sound alone.

    How to

    1. Hit Ping. The built-in synthetic echo path draws instantly and returns a known distance first run, no room setup needed.
    2. Rotate (or drag the bearing slider) and ping again to paint another point on the top-down map.
    3. Optionally enable mic capture for a real echo trace from your room on top of the self-test path.

    Local: python3 -m http.server in the repo root, then open localhost:8000/showcase/demos/browser-sonar/ (audio + crypto.subtle need localhost/https, not file://).
