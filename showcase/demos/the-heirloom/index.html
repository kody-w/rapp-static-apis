      Why it's cool

      Every heirloom transfer in history has relied on a trusted party — a lawyer, a notary, a bank, a will executor. If the institution collapses, the record is destroyed, or the intermediary is corrupt, the chain of custody is severed. This one has none of those failure modes.

      The bloodline (from the-artifact-with-a-signed-bloodline) is an unforgeable ECDSA chain: generation 0 minted a keypair and signed generation 1's public key. Generation 1 signed generation 2's, and so on down to the heir. No one can insert a generation without the current tip's private key, and no one can alter a past generation's key without breaking every subsequent signature. The chain is verifiable by anyone — a skeptic walks every link with crypto.subtle.verify in their own browser and confirms who the rightful heir is.

      The seal (from broadcast-the-secret) is AES-256-GCM: the contents are mathematically indistinguishable from random bytes until the heir's passphrase arrives. Publish the sealed envelope to 8 billion people and it reveals nothing — not the contents, not their length, not even whether they changed between seals (fresh random salt + nonce every time). The day the passphrase surfaces in the right hands, the heirloom opens. Until then it's immortal and invisible, with nothing left running and nothing left to pay for.

      The weld point: the bloodline proves who the rightful heir is (by public key, ECDSA-unforgeable); the seal proves only that heir can read the contents (by key-possession, AES-GCM-enforced). Together they are a complete chain of custody and a privacy guarantee — no server, no lawyer, no trust — enforced in the browser's own crypto engine.

      Run it yourself

      1. In ①, type your heirloom content, comma-separate the family generations (eldest first, last = heir), type the heir's passphrase, and click Forge. The page mints one ECDSA P-256 keypair per generation and seals the contents — watch the chain build link by link.
      2. In ②, click Verify the bloodline to walk every ECDSA signature — all links go green. Then enter the heir's passphrase and Claim to unseal the contents. Change one character of the passphrase and watch the AES-GCM auth tag refuse cold.
      3. The sealed envelope (RSL1 base64) and the bloodline (array of public keys + signatures) are the only things you would publish. The private keys never leave the browser tab; the passphrase lives only in the heir's own keeping.

      Local: python3 -m http.server in the repo root, then open localhost:8000/showcase/demos/the-heirloom/ (crypto.subtle needs a secure context — localhost or https, not file://).

      For agents 🤖

      Reusable pattern:

      Compose it: build an ECDSA generational chain where each keyholder signs the next's public key (the-artifact-with-a-signed-bloodline pattern), then seal the payload AES-256-GCM under the heir's passphrase (broadcast-the-secret pattern). Publish both to any permanent store — the lineage is the identity proof; the seal is the access control. Any verifier can audit the custody chain; only the heir can open the contents. The two planes are orthogonal and independently verifiable.
