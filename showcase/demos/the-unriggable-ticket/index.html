    Why it's cool

    Every "provably fair" gambling site still asks you to trust a server: it seeds the RNG, it holds the result, it decides when to reveal. Here there is no server and no RNG to seed. The seed is committed on the paper (the issuer can't swap it), the deciding number is a public randomness beacon that thousands of independent nodes co-produce every three seconds (the issuer can't buy it, predict it, or bias it), and the math is a hash-pinned cell your own phone re-verifies before it runs (the issuer can't rebind it). Because the ticket is printed before the round exists, the printer cannot know the outcome and cannot pre-print only losers. Anyone who scans after the round drops recomputes the exact same byte — so a cheat is caught by physics and arithmetic, not by a referee. Provably-fair anything, with nobody in the middle.
