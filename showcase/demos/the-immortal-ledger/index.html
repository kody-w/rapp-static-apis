    Why it's cool

    Kill the Host proves the compute cell's real identity is its content hash, so any surviving fork or mirror can keep serving it. Proof of Freshness proves each ledger entry was minted from exact bytes fetched now, not a stale screenshot. Chain of Custody proves every entry descends from the prior one, so one changed byte snaps the lineage.

    The weld point is that freshness proofs stop being isolated receipts: they become the content of an append-only hash chain whose verifier itself survives host death. So the ledger can move hosts, prove its whole bloodline, and keep appending heads without any backend at all.

    Run it yourself

    1. Click Mint a fresh immortal ledger. You should see the dead origin crossed out, the survivor mirror answer from the pinned cell, and three fresh ledger entries minted from live byte proofs.
    2. Copy the shown fetched_at and sha256(response_bytes) values. Re-run: timestamps advance and hashes change whenever the live payload changes.
    3. Click Flip one byte in entry 2. The chain verifier should fail at that exact hop because the stored hash no longer matches the mutated content.

    Local: python3 -m http.server in the repo root, then open localhost:8000/showcase/demos/the-immortal-ledger/ (crypto.subtle needs localhost or https, not file://).
