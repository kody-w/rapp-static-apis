    Why it's cool

    Kill the Host proves that the feed's compute cell is identified by its content hash, not its host — delete the origin and a surviving mirror keeps the broadcast alive. Proof of Freshness proves that each emitted value was fetched at an exact moment and binds it to the SHA-256 of the exact bytes received, so nobody can pass off a stale snapshot as a live reading.

    The weld point is that censorship-resistance and replay-resistance become inseparable: a broadcast that survives its host dying is only trustworthy if each emission is also freshness-stamped. Kill the origin — the feed keeps broadcasting from a mirror. Try to replay an old emission as current — the freshness proof exposes it. Neither guarantee alone is enough; together they make a verifiable broadcast.

    Run it yourself

    1. Click 📡 Broadcast from mirrors. You'll see the origin crossed out, surviving mirrors resolving the same pinned cell, and fresh broadcast items appearing with their SHA-256 freshness proofs.
    2. Copy a fetched_at timestamp and sha256(response_bytes). Click again — timestamps advance and hashes change whenever the live source changes.
    3. Click 👻 Replay a stale broadcast — the freshness check shows the replay's timestamp is in the past and flags the emission as stale. This is the replay attack the proof protects against.

    Local: python3 -m http.server in the repo root, then open localhost:8000/showcase/demos/the-verifiable-broadcast/ (crypto.subtle needs localhost or https, not file://).
