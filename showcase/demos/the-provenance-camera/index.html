    Why it's cool

    Most QR codes are just shortcuts to a URL. This one is a pedigree. The moment you press Capture, the content is hashed with crypto.subtle — a browser-native cryptographic primitive — producing a genesis hash that is mathematically bound to those exact bytes. A custody entry then links the content hash to the genesis, forming a content-addressed chain (the same logic chain-of-custody proved for full document timelines). The resulting QR carries that chain as its payload: scan it and you have everything needed to re-verify the lineage from scratch.

    This is what chain-of-custody proved about tamper-evident history, and what photosynthesis proved about living QR offspring, combined into a single act: a fact enters, a tamper-evident pedigree exits — scannable, self-contained, no server anywhere in the loop.

    Run it yourself

    1. Type a short fact (sensor reading, note, content hash) and click Capture & stamp.
    2. The QR drawn on screen encodes the full provenance record as compact JSON. Scan it with any QR reader — you get genesis hash, custody entry, and head hash, enough to re-verify the chain.
    3. Change even one character in the fact and press Capture again — you get a completely different genesis hash and QR, proving the binding is exact.

    Local: python3 -m http.server in the repo root, then open localhost:8000/showcase/demos/the-provenance-camera/ (crypto.subtle needs a secure context — localhost or https, not file://).
