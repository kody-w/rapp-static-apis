    Why it's cool

    A printed square on a coffee bag has no CPU, no battery, no network. Yet the moment a shopper points a phone at it, their browser becomes the computer: it pulls a tiny content-addressed program, proves it wasn't tampered with (the SHA-256 must match the pin, or it refuses to run), and reaches a free public weather API for the exact hillside the beans grew on. The bag "knows" it's raining there — live — with no server anyone pays for and nothing to shut down. Swap the coordinates and it's any farm, any river, any sky.

    Make your own · run it yourself

    1. Open /track, pick the Seattle temp/Open-Meteo preset, swap in your farm's lat/long, set a path like current.temperature_2m.
    2. Hit Make QR — it encodes track/run.html#<spec> with the zero-dependency encoder.
    3. Print it on the bag. Anyone who scans it runs the same verified cell in their own browser. Free, global, forever.

    Local: python3 -m http.server in the repo root, then open localhost:8000/showcase/demos/taste-the-weather/ (crypto.subtle needs a secure context — localhost or https, not file://).
