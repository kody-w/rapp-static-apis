          Why it's cool

          Each donor solves one slice of the problem. Welding them creates something none had alone: a zero-backend storefront where live inventory is always fresh (Issues labels), orders are mathematically sealed (AES-256-GCM — only the seller's passphrase can open them, the math says no to everyone else), and the sealed envelope is content-addressed into a custody chain so any in-transit tampering is instantly provable. Three planes of trust — freshness, confidentiality, and provenance — all running in the buyer's browser with nothing to deploy and nothing to breach.

          The weld point is what makes this more than the sum of three demos: the RSL1 base64 blob (the sealed order) is exactly the content that gets SHA-256 stamped into the custody chain. A seller verifies the custody chain to confirm the sealed order wasn't altered between submission and receipt, then decrypts with their key. Two independent cryptographic checks on the same bytes — integrity and confidentiality together — and the only thing that runs them is your own browser.

          Run it yourself

          1. Press Check what's available — the live menu loads from GitHub Issues labels in real time.
          2. Select at least one available item, optionally add a buyer note, then press Seal & submit order.
          3. Watch the RSL1 envelope form byte-by-byte, then see it SHA-256 stamped into a custody link.
          4. Press Verify full custody chain — the browser replays every hash link from the genesis anchor.
          5. To mark an item sold out: open a GitHub Issue on kody-w/rapp-static-apis and add a label sold-out:<item-key>. Refresh the menu to see it disappear.
          • sold-out:margherita-pizza
          • sold-out:mushroom-ramen
          • sold-out:halloumi-tacos
          • sold-out:charred-broccolini
          • sold-out:citrus-tonic

          Local: python3 -m http.server in the repo root, then open localhost:8000/showcase/demos/the-sealed-storefront/ (crypto.subtle needs HTTPS or localhost, not file://).
