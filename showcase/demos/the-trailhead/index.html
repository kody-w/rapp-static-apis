    Why it's cool

    A trailhead post is ordinary wood and metal. The sticker on it is just vinyl. But a quick scan can still be kind: your phone runs one tiny verified cell, asks one public weather feed for one specific place, and turns that into one clear sentence for this exact moment. It's low drama and genuinely useful.

    Make your own · run it yourself

    1. Open /track, pick an Open-Meteo preset, and swap in your trail's lat/long.
    2. Set a single dotted path (like current.temperature_2m) and click Make QR.
    3. Print the QR at the trailhead. Every scan runs the same hash-verified extract cell in the scanner's browser.

    Local: python3 -m http.server in the repo root, then open localhost:8000/showcase/demos/the-trailhead/ (crypto.subtle needs a secure context — localhost or https, not file://).
