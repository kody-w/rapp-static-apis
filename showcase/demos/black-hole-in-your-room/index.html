The Black Hole In Your Room

Live — run it right now

Press #go. This instantly paints a lensed universe on the canvas, then tries to upgrade to your real camera feed. Tilt your phone to anchor and steer the black hole in space; tilt forward to make the horizon swell and hear the rumble rise.

Watch what's happening

    Why it's cool

    A static page is swallowing your real room in real time. The camera stream (or a built-in nebula if no camera is usable) is turned into a GPU texture, then a handwritten fragment shader bends those photons around a dark core using a Schwarzschild-style deflection field. You get the look of gravitational lensing, an accretion disk with Doppler tint, and a photon ring rim — all from one browser tab, no app, no backend.

    How to

    1. Press #go; the first frame appears immediately with synthetic stars/nebula.
    2. Allow camera access to lens your real room. If denied, fake, or unavailable, it stays in synthetic space mode and still works.
    3. Tilt your phone to shift anchor and depth: left/right moves the hole, forward tilt expands the horizon and deepens the bass.

    Local: python3 -m http.server in the repo root, then open localhost:8000/showcase/demos/black-hole-in-your-room/ (camera/sensors need https or localhost).
