    Why it's cool

    This is not curve fitting in the usual sense. The browser builds a candidate library of physical terms (x, xdot, sin(x), x^2, constant), computes derivatives from the tracked signal, and uses sparse regression (sequentially-thresholded least squares) to discover the smallest differential equation that explains the motion. Then solve_ivp projects forward and draws where the mass should be. A static page just inferred a law of motion and challenged reality in real time.

    How to

    1. Press #go to run the built-in synthetic pendulum. The observed signal appears instantly; Python analysis continues in the background.
    2. Wait for the recovered equation to print in the Live box, then compare magenta prediction vs cyan real future trace on the canvas.
    3. Optionally enable camera mode, point at a pendulum-like moving object, and run again to identify a law from your real scene.

    Local: python3 -m http.server in the repo root, then open localhost:8000/showcase/demos/the-law-thief/ (Pyodide + crypto.subtle require localhost or https).
