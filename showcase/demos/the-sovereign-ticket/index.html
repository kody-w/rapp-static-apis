WELD: The Sovereign Ticket

Live — mint it, wait for the beacon, redeem it at the door

This is the full welded flow: the issuer signs the exact ticket payload, commits its redeem proof to a future drand round, then seals that signed packet behind a 2-of-2 XOR split key. One share rides on the holder's phone, the other lives on the venue scanner. Redemption succeeds only when the round has dropped and both shares meet in the same browser.

    Why it's cool

    This keeps all three donors alive. From The Unbreakable Promise it transplants the in-browser ECDSA loop, so the issuer signs the exact holder + event + round + seed bytes and any forged edit fails verification. From The Unriggable Ticket it transplants the public-beacon trick, so the redeem proof is tied to a drand round that does not exist yet at issuance time. From Only in the Same Frame it transplants the XOR-split AES key, so the signed packet is not even readable until the holder's phone and the venue's scanner meet.

    That means the ticket is simultaneously authentic, unpredictable, and physically co-present. The page also keeps a tiny browser-side spend ledger so the same verified ticket digest is refused on a second scan in the same door verifier.

    Run it yourself

    1. Press Mint a fresh sovereign ticket. The page verifies a pinned extract cell, signs the exact payload, learns the current drand head, commits the ticket to a future round, then seals the signed receipt into two shares.
    2. Leave both shares in place and press Redeem at the door. If the beacon round has not dropped yet, the page waits until it does, then computes the redeem proof in your browser and spends that ticket digest into the local door ledger.
    3. Enable simulate a forged signature to watch ECDSA verification refuse, or simulate the wrong venue share to watch the AES-GCM seal refuse before any signature check can even happen.

    Local: python3 -m http.server in the repo root, then open localhost:8000/showcase/demos/the-sovereign-ticket/ (crypto.subtle needs localhost or https, not file://).
