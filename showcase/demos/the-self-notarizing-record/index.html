WELD: The Self-Notarizing Record

Live — notarize the record and replay its whole history

This weld keeps both donors alive: the record continuously recomputes its own current SHA-256 like The Page That Proves Itself, while every saved revision keeps the full parent-linked custody replay from Chain of Custody. The weld point is the save itself: each save promotes the freshly computed self-hash into the next custody link.

    Why it's cool

    Most integrity checks tell you only whether the bytes in front of you are current. Most history checks tell you only whether the lineage behind you is intact. This record does both at once: the browser re-hashes the live text you are reading right now, and every save turns that exact self-hash into the next custody link in an append-only chain.

    That means the present can say "I still match the published head", while the past can say "every prior revision still descends from genesis". Edit the current draft and the head no longer matches. Edit an old revision and the chain names the exact broken version.

    Run it yourself

    1. Click Verify current bytes + history. You should get a green verdict: the live text matches the latest published head and every version replays from genesis.
    2. Edit the record text without saving, then verify again. The history stays intact, but the current self-hash no longer matches the published head.
    3. Click Save as next notarized version. The new self-hash becomes the next custody link and the page verifies green again.
    4. Click Alter version 2 after publish. Verification fails exactly where the historical byte change breaks the chain.

    Local: python3 -m http.server in the repo root, then open localhost:8000/showcase/demos/the-self-notarizing-record/ (crypto.subtle needs localhost or https, not file://).
