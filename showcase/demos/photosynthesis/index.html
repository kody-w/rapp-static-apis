    Why it's cool

    A printed square has no CPU, no battery, no network — it just sits there like a leaf. Then a stranger points a phone at it and their browser becomes the chloroplast: it pulls a tiny content-addressed program, proves it wasn't tampered with (the SHA-256 must match the pin, or it refuses to run), and reaches a free market API for the live Bitcoin price. That's the light going in.

    Then the trick that makes it photosynthesis and not just a reading: the same page imports the inlined, zero-dependency qr.mjs encoder and draws a new QR — a seed. And the seed isn't a photo of the number; it encodes a track/run.html tracker spec. Scan the child and a different stranger's browser boots the same hash-verified cell and re-pulls the same live value. The artifact consumed live data and, from it, grew a working, self-contained replica of itself — with no backend anywhere in the loop.

    Make your own · run it yourself

    1. Open /track, pick the BTC / USD preset (or any free CORS-open source + dotted path).
    2. Hit Make QR — it encodes track/run.html#<spec> with the same qr.mjs this page uses.
    3. Print it, sticker it, tattoo it. Every scan runs the verified cell in a fresh browser — and this page shows how one artifact can also emit the next one.

    Local: python3 -m http.server in the repo root, then open localhost:8000/showcase/demos/photosynthesis/ (crypto.subtle needs a secure context — localhost or https, not file://).
